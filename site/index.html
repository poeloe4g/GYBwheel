What is this? (plain-English guide)

This page screens for cash-secured puts — a conservative options trade where you get paid cash today for promising to buy a high-quality stock at a discount price (the strike) if it falls that far by a set date. If the stock stays above the strike, you simply keep the cash. If it falls below, you buy shares you already wanted — at the discount — and can sell "covered calls" against them (the "wheel").

Every weekday the screener scans large, profitable companies and lists contracts that pass its safety checks: no earnings announcement before the expiry date, a reasonable price cushion, decent trading volume, and a premium worth the wait. It never trades — it only suggests, and you decide.

  • Traffic light (top): whether overall market conditions favor opening new trades at all.
  • Today's ideas: the ranked table. Higher score = better premium for the risk taken.
  • Near misses: ideas that failed exactly one or two checks — shown so you can see why they were excluded.
  • Track record: once suggested contracts expire, this page grades its own past suggestions.

Educational tool only. Options involve risk; you can lose money — including being obligated to buy shares that keep falling. Nothing here is investment advice.

Track record

Once a suggested contract's expiry date passes, it is graded: WIN means the stock stayed above the strike, so the seller kept the full premium; BREACH means it closed below and the seller would own the shares (the return shown assumes buying at the strike and marks the loss at the expiry-day price). Near misses are graded too — if excluded ideas win just as often as accepted ones, a safety check may be too strict.
